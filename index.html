Sant Tulsidas

Sant Tulsidas was not always a saint; he was once a man deeply entangled in the web of worldly passion. His legendary obsession with his wife, Ratnavali, led him to cross a flooded river at midnight just to catch a glimpse of her. It was in this moment of extreme human desire that Ratnavali spoke the words that would birth a master: "If you loved Rama with even half the intensity you feel for this body of bone and skin, you would have crossed the ocean of existence."

"If you loved the Divine with the same intensity you feel for this body, you would be realized in an instant."

This spiritual shock redirected Tulsidasâ€™s life. He surrendered entirely to Sri Rama, eventually composing the Ramcharitmanas. Goda Devi (Andal)

Andal is the only female Alvar among the twelve great Vaishnava saints of South India. Found as a baby under a Tulsi plant in Srivilliputhur, she is revered as the incarnation of Bhudevi (Mother Earth). Her devotion was uniqueâ€”it was a 'sovereign claim' over the Divine. She refused to marry any man, declaring that she belonged only to Lord Ranganatha. Her name 'Andal' means 'the one who ruled' the Lord with her love.

She is most celebrated for the Tiruppavai, 30 stanzas that call upon souls to wake up and seek the grace of Lord Narayana. Saint Sakkubai

Sakkubai's life is a testimony to the power of unwavering endurance. A devotee of Lord Vitthala, she faced extreme hardship in her household. Her devotion was so magnetic that the Lord Himself took her form to perform her chores so she could visit Pandharpur. Through Sakkubai, we learn that devotion doesn't require us to escape our duties, but to do them with a heart fixed on the Divine.

Saint Kabir

Kabir, the weaver saint of Varanasi, stripped away the complexities of religion to reveal the simple Truth. He taught that the Divine is not in temples or mosques, but within the honest heart. His Dohas are spiritual arrows that pierce the ego. Kabir lived a life of total integrity, showing that one can be a householder and a mystic simultaneously.
